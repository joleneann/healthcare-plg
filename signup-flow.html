PLG Strategy: Good Friction

Profiling before sandbox is intentional. Counterintuitively, adding "good friction" early outperforms minimizing time-to-value. Anthropic's onboarding asks who you are and what your interests are before product access - critics call the flow long, but the data supports it. Profiling here serves two purposes: (1) the sandbox experience is tailored by industry + role signals, creating stronger psychological ownership, and (2) users who self-select feel the product is "for them" before they touch it. Compound returns: early profiling pays beyond activation - it enables segment-specific lifecycle marketing (drip sequences by role), feeds lookalike targeting for paid acquisition, and reduces churn by ensuring users land in the right product surface from day one. Progressive reveal shows one question at a time as pills. Research: cascading flows convert 86% higher (HubSpot). Industry + Role power module recommendation. Company size + Title power PQL scoring.

PLG Strategy: Post-Value Email Gate

Email is asked after profiling + sandbox activation, not before. The user has self-identified (profiling) and experienced value (sandbox), so the ask feels earned - conversion is 3x higher than upfront gates. SSO buttons (Google/Microsoft) serve dual purpose: convenience + enterprise detection. A @largeclinic.org Microsoft login is a PQL signal. Personal email is accepted with a smaller cap.
